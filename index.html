Personal Statement

My work experience and research share two common themes: people and technology. I have engaged in applied research while operating in agile software development environments at both MIT and Nuance. Academically, I have conducted basic research, which may lead to published work (currently under review) in the Journal of Human Factors. In addition to this, I gained valuable experience presenting our research at national and regional conferences around the U.S., while working at Fisher College and the University of Central Florida.

Traversing the fields of psychology, engineering, and technology has inspired me to pursue graduate studies in the field of Human Factors at Tufts University. I strive to apply my expertise in research and development to create elegant and intuitive systems or products for people to use.

When I'm not building digital things I enjoy weight-lifting, yoga, meditation, hiking, surfing, snowboarding, traveling, scuba diving, and playing music.
